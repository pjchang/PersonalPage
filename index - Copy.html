The Moral Machine

http://moralmachine.mit.edu/

#visualization #interactivewebdesign #machineethics #crowdsourcing #trolleyproblem

From self-driving cars on public roads to self-piloting reusable rockets landing on self-sailing ships, machine intelligence is supporting or entirely taking over ever more complex human activities at an ever increasing pace. The greater autonomy given machine intelligence in these roles can result in situations where they have to make autonomous choices involving human life and limb. This calls for not just a clearer understanding of how humans make such choices, but also a clearer understanding of how humans perceive machine intelligence making such choices.

Recent scientific studies on machine ethics have raised awareness about the topic in the media and public discourse. We aim to take the discussion further, by creating a platform for 1) building a crowd-sourced picture of human opinion on how machines should make decisions when faced with moral dilemmas, and 2) crowd-sourcing assembly and discussion of potential scenarios of moral consequence.

To this end, our team develops ‘The Moral Machine’, a platform that engages users in moral dilemmas and asks them how the self-driving car should respond. A handful of factors play into each scenario, including the age and gender of victims, their social status, and whether they are breaking the law. Participants are asked to make decisions in 13 dilemmas. The results then pooled as crowdsourcing data and may be used to guide the development of ethical machines. After judging the dilemmas, users can compare their outcomes to others’ and even design their own for others to answer.

I am one of the three main developers in the team, focusing on both frontend and backend design and programming using Meteor.js web framework, MongoDB, d3.js, jQuery, JavaScript and others. More information can be obtained by surfing our website. This program has been widely reported by New York Times, Guardian, The Times, Newsweek, and other media.

The Skillscape

http://scalable.media.mit.edu/skillscape/ (PW: workforce)

#visualization #interactivedesign #socialnetworks #jobsautomation

The labor market is one of the fundamental forces in the economy. At its core, the labor force is built on the skills, knowledge, and abilities of workers. The competitiveness of those workers depends on their ability to adapt to changing conditions, from computerization and industrial automation, to changes in labor demand.

We define a parameter called Revealed Comparative Advantage (RCA) as the ratio between the proportion of a particular skill in one job and the proportion of that skill across all jobs. An RCA value being greater than 1 indicates that skill is more important to the job, compared to all other jobs. We then define the skill space network by first finding a maximum spanning tree in the proximity matrix between each pair of skills and then linking up any paired skills with a proximity value greater than 0.8.

Finally, we create an online platform called ‘The Skillscape’ for helping individuals and policy-makers understand the composition of various jobs or entire labor markets. It allows us to unpack the skills inside different jobs, and to visualize how these skills relate to each other. By mapping entire cities into the skills landscape, policy-makers can understand how different cities compare in terms of their labor force's underlying skill composition. This has implications on the city's ability to compete with other cities, and to respond to external shocks, such as computerization and automation of various skills.

I am one of the two main developers in the team, focusing on interactive web design using d3.js, jQuery, JavaScript library, Bootstrap and others. More information can be obtained by surfing our website.

The GlobalEyes

#visualization #interactivedesign #politicalscience #massmedia

Recent improvements in computational linguistics, data processing, and storage have made it possible to automatically extract high resolution data about political events directly from the news. This offers new possibilities for studying international political interaction networks, which have traditionally relied on data about formal alliances and trade among nations. We believe new Tools should be created to help tackle such complex problems such as understanding international relationships. They could lead the way towards better understanding and thus more informed decision-making.

Therefore, by applying machine-coded event dataset collected by Global Database of Events, Language and Tone (GDELT), we create a platform called ‘The GlobalEyes’ which is constructed to serve as an integrated toolkit for visualization of interactions among countries of the world over time. Incorporating classic visualizations as well as innovative, in-house research tools, it is designed with the needs of political scientists, historians, journalists, and the public in mind. It is able to reflect the temporal and regional trends of relationship between countries and offer several possible explanations of those changes.

Each visualization is equipped with numerous intuitive controls for filtering and drilldown, making for a thoroughly interactive experience of exploring the rich and constantly growing datasets upon which it is built.

I am the main developer on this project, focusing mainly frontend web development by using d3.js and JavaScript library, and partial backend development, using Spark and its SQL.

It is still a work-in-progress. We still need more time to figure out how to handle large user requests due to the huge dataset we have (about 60 millions). However, the demo link is provided so it might give you a sense of how this platform should work.

Misery Loves Company: Happiness and Communication in the City

Published in EPJ Data Science 2015 4:7

#datascience #socialmedia #sentimentanalysis #networking

The high population density in cities confers many advantages, including improved social interaction and information exchange. However, it is often argued that urban living comes at the expense of reducing happiness. Motivated by recent results on the geography of happiness and the communication structure in cities, we are interested in understanding the relationship between urban happiness and urban communication.

We then analyze geo-located social media posts (tweets) within a major urban center (Milan) to produce a detailed spatial map of urban sentiments. We combine this data with high-resolution mobile communication intensity data among different urban areas.

Our results reveal that happy (respectively unhappy) areas preferentially communicate with other areas of their type. There is an evidence of assortative mixing (homophily) in communication between different urban areas based on their happiness level. We also found that the mean of happiness seems to vary with community size, where community is defined in terms of communication structure.

In this project, I am working as a research analytics, using the network tools (ex:Gephi and python NetworkX), statistic programs and their library (ex: R, Python, Pandas, Numpy) for calculating sentiment score for each tweet, applying multi-level modularity optimization algorithm on communication network and visualizing the results.

Temporal Trend of International Relations from Event Database

Presented at Netsci 2015

#datascience #massmedia #locationbasedsocialmedia #internationalrelations

Global Database of Events, Language and Tone (GDELT), including more than 200 million dyadic real-worlds events from 1979-2015, offers geographic event information along with actor categorization. In this work, we reconstructed the international relations in each year by aggregating event data and discussed its’ temporal trends. Two measurement of international relations were applied in our analysis: number of event(#event), which was reflected as the strength of international relations, and Weighted Goldstein, which indicated as the cooperation-and-conflict scale of relations

We applied non-normalized Shannon’s entropy analysis on #event distribution for countries over time and observed the dramatic increase of entropy by year. Entropy change would reveal two facts of relations: connectivity and equality. It is proved that the world becomes equally connected over years.Also, we aggregated the Weighted Goldstein of international relations toward same country by average and defined it as the measurement the incoming attitude. The incoming attitude indicates the global attitude from other countries to specific country. If the global attitude tends to be cooperative, the incoming attitude is positive; If the attitude tends to be conflicted, the value is negative. Then, we observed the difference of average incoming attitude in recently 18 years to previous 18 years and discovered the decreasing tendency for overall pattern while increasing tendency on those countries which recently become more economically developed. For example, India, China and Russia, which obtain friendlier attitude from other countries, are a member of ”BRIC”. The increasing tendency would be a reflection of economic growth.

Finally, for top 30 countries which obtain the most relations, we applied cosine similarity analysis on Weighted Goldstein to analyze the similarity of international relations among every country pairs. Also, hierarchical clustering analysis was applied for detecting cluster. The result reveals the international leagues with adverse attitude toward each other was found in recently years. Country shows the similar pattern of relation with member in the same league while show opposite pattern of relations with other league member. Also, the stronger cluster was found in the recent years.

This work is also my master thesis topic at Masdar Institute.

Method and experiments of subliminal cueing for real-world images

Published in Multimedia Tools Appl. 74(22):10111-10135

#visualattention #humanvisusalsystem #eyetracking #subliminalcue

Unconscious attention shift triggered by a subliminal cue has been shown to be automatic; however, whether it can be brought into effect for images of real-world scenes remains to be investigated.

In this work, we are concerned with the guidance of human visual attention without involving the viewers’ awareness. Such a technique can make, for example, a digital signage advertisement more effective because it pulls and holds the viewers’ attention while avoiding top-down controlled resistance in human perception as discovered in scientific research. Therefore, we present a subliminal cueing method that flashes briefly a visual cue before presenting a real-world image to the viewer. The effectiveness of the method is verified by experiments using three types of cues (spatial cue, face cue, and object cue) of varied durations.

The Results show three types of cues have different properties on the ability to shift viewers’ attention. One is a spatial cue and it is able to direct attention to the cued hemifield. The other two types of cues require face can direct attention to the precise cued location.

The experiments demonstrate that a brief subliminal cue presented prior to the color image of a real-world complex scene can attract human visual attention. The method is useful for many applications that require efficient, unresisting attention shift to a target image area.

In this project, I am in charge with the object cue section. My works includes imagine signal processing on MATLAB, the eye-tracker system operation, and applying ANOVA test via using SPSS.
